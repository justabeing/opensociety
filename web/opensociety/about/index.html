Our Philosophy

Many people do not know about the hardships of feeling alone. Many people have family and friends in their lives, people that make them feel good. People they love, and people that loves them in return. People that help and support them through life. It's a closed circle. When there's a party, the same people come. The only exception being someone bringing their friends along. All these new people have already established value and trust with the group through their connection.

There is nothing wrong with a closed group. But there are those who are alone, and those who need help. These people are the poor doomed. The outcasts. The young, the silly, the honest and weak. They're doomed, they're lost, they're helpless. They're somebody else's meal. They're like pigs in the wilderness.

Open Society is a community for all people.

As a member of Open Society and Burning Man Stockholm you will be a part of a community that brings people together to explore and realize ideas. Open Society is an experiment in community, technology, art, self-expression and self-reliance. It is influenced by great movements and events such as the hippies, Woodstock and Burning Man.

In this group we try to find ways to feel better and create the change necessary in all different areas and systems of society.

Our philosophy is to not exercise authority over anyone but ourself and our own bodies. Therefore violence and hurting people is against the basic idea of our community. We do not say what you as a member can and cannot do. Instead we're open to anyone and any activity that share our philosophy. We help others and if we cannot help, we do not hurt them.

Anything you see on this website or in our group is not set in stone and may change over time as the community learns new things and develops. This kind of fluidity and openness to necessary change is also in the center of our way of thinking.

The community does not yet have any rules in the basic sense. This is because a rule may imply a majority exercising authority over a minority that does not agree with the rule. Instead we work with protocols and standards for communication. We try to solve problems and conflicts with ideas and dialogue. And we choose to look for solutions in ways other than just focusing on the problem.

The first version of our protocol stand as follows:


Protocol #1:

If you cannot help someone, do not hurt them

The golden rule according to his holiness the 14th Dalai Lama.

No authority over anyone but yourself

Help and cooperate with others. If there is conflict, communicate your feelings and find solutions.

Take your time and do things in your own pace

Let others and yourself take the time you need to do things. Find yourself and help others if they want your help.

Listen to others

Truly listen to others and show compassion, kindness and respect.


Members of the group can make changes to this protocol and create their own version. This personal protocol can then be approved by and seen as a contract with the rest of the group that is used for interactions with the member.

When signing up to the group you should either accept Protocol #1 above or create your own version. This protocol will then act as a basic contract of interaction between you and the rest of the community.

When the community grows the protocols, or contracts, will become larger in number and more complex. There will be standard protocols for different type of interactions that people share. Many of these contracts can be written as smart contracts to automatically execute, control or document legally relevant events and actions. In this way we can drastically decrease bureaucracy and legal resources in our society.

If you have any ideas how we can improve or a project you would like to share and develop with us, please use the sign-up form to write a little about your idea or project.
