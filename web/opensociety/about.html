Our Philosophy

As a member of Open Society you will be a part of a community that bring people together to explore and realize ideas. Open Society is an experiment in community, society, art and technology influenced by festivals such as Woodstock and Burning Man. The community is organizing the first Burning Man like experiment in Stockholm where we together as a community try to find ways to feel better, improve society and build change.

Our philosophy is to not exercise authority over anyone but ourself and our own bodies. Therefore violence and hurting people is against the basic idea of our community. We do not say what you as a member can or cannot do. Instead we are open to anyone and any activity that share our philosophy. We help others and if we cannot help, we do not hurt them.

Anything you see on this website or in our group is not set in stone and may change over time as the community learn new things and develop. This kind of fluidity and openness to necessary changes is also in the center of our way of thinking.

If you have any ideas how we can improve or a project you would like to share and develop with us, please use the sign-up form to write a little about your idea or project.


The community does not yet have any rules in the basic sense. This is because a rule may imply a majority exercising authority over a minority that does not agree to the rule. Instead we work with protocols and standards for communication. And try to solve problems and conflicts with ideas and communication.
